Leonardo da Vinci

The man who revolutionized art and science forever

Here's a time line of Leonardo da Vinci's life:

  • April 15, 1452 - Leonardo da Vinci is born in Vinci, Italy, to a notary and a peasant woman.
  • 1466-1476 - Leonardo is an apprentice in the studio of Andrea del Verrocchio in Florence, where he learns painting, sculpture, and technical skills such as metalworking and mechanics.
  • 1482-1499 - Leonardo works for the Duke of Milan as an artist and engineer, creating works such as The Last Supper and designing military weaponry and machines.
  • 1500-1506 - Leonardo returns to Florence and works on a series of paintings, including the famous Mona Lisa.
  • 1506-1513 - Leonardo travels between Florence, Venice, and Rome, working on various projects and receiving commissions from wealthy patrons.
  • 1516-1519 - Leonardo moves to France, where he becomes a court artist for King Francis I and continues to work on scientific projects until his death.
  • May 2, 1519 - Leonardo da Vinci dies in Amboise, France, at the age of 67.
